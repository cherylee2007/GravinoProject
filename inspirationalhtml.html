About Me

Introduction

Hello, I am Cherylee Gravino, a highly motivated first-year student majoring in Computer Science at Tangub City Global College. I am a Lux Mundis who is passionate about learning, culture, and art. Writing this reflection helps me understand myself better and share my journey with others.


Personal Background

Growing up in a close-knit community has taught me the importance of resilience and cooperation. My family and surroundings shaped my values of respect, hard work, and care for others.Education has always been central to my journey. From early schooling, I developed a curiosity for technology, art, and philosophy, which later grew into a passion for learning english language well.

Beyond academics, everyday experiences—helping at home, participating in local events, and engaging with peers—have influenced how I see myself. These moments taught me responsibility, empathy, and the importance of community involvement.


Values and Beliefs

I believe that knowledge should be accessible to everyone. I value clarity, honesty, and creativity in both learning and communication. And as a former student of Humanities and Social Sciences, for me Philosophy and Filipino culture inspire me to see the world in different perspectives.


Strengths & Skills

- Breaking down complex ideas into simple explanations
- Academic Writings
- Effective Communication Skills
- Public Speaking
- Critical Thinking and so on

Challenges & Growth

One of my biggest challenges has been struggled with balancing multiple responsibilities—academic work, personal commitments, and creative interests. There were moments when I felt overwhelmed, but these experiences helped me develop time management skills and the ability to prioritize what truly matters.

Another challenge has been self-doubt. There were times when I questioned whether my ideas were good enough or whether I could meet expectations. Overcoming this required building confidence in my abilities and trusting the process of learning. Each small success reminded me that growth comes step by step.

I’ve also faced difficulties in expressing complex ideas clearly, especially in academic writing. Through practice, feedback, and reflection, I’ve grown more comfortable in simplifying concepts and making them accessible to others. This challenge became one of my strengths.
